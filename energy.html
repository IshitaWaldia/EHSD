Introduction

Human Energy Harvesting System (HRES)
Nowadays, gymnasiums are equipped with indoor cycling equipment and cardio equipment that captures the energy from your workout and turns it into electricity to power the building.

The specified mechanism is a hybrid source, combined with a solar panel and a vibrator floor that generates electricity from your footsteps. The total accumulation of the combined energy is stored in a battery and, in the future, will be converted back using a micro-inverter into utility-grade electricity to power electrical appliances.

Features

  • Harvest energy from gym shoe motion:

    A robust system that utilizes piezoelectric sensors embedded in gym shoes to efficiently capture the mechanical energy generated by individuals during activities like jumping and running.

  • Convert mechanical energy for device charging:

    Integrated circuitry that converts the mechanical energy from piezoelectric sensors into electrical energy, enabling the charging of mobile devices such as cell phones.

  • Combine energy from indoor gym equipment:

    A hybrid energy generation system combining energy captured from indoor gym equipment, including cycling machines and cardio equipment.

  • Display real-time energy data for awareness:

    A user-friendly interface that displays real-time energy generation and usage, helping users and gyms track how much energy they are generating.
